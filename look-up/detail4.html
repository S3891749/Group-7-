Diabetes

Details

Diabetes is a long-term disease that happens when the pancreas doesn't make enough insulin or when the body doesn't use the insulin it makes well enough. High blood sugar, or hyperglycemia, is caused by diabetes. This is bad for many parts of the body, including the nerves and blood vessels. Cardiovascular manifestations: Angina (pain with pressure between the sternum, often spreading to the chin and shoulder, left hand, shortness of breath, and may be accompanied by sweating or fainting), fatigue on exertion strength, purple. For cerebrovascular disease, there may be 3 signs of stroke: Weakness in the side of the body or limbs, slurred speech or meaningless words, and distorted mouth.


Treatment & Prevention

There are many ways you can control and prevent diabetes as follows: Weight control Increase physical activity Eat healthy vegetables Eat healthy fats Avoid fast dieting No smoking Drink alcohol in moderation Check your sugar regularly
